ABOUT THE PROJECT

Hello! I'm Katya Little, a student in the Growth and Structure of Cities department at Bryn Mawr College. This website and the maps on it are my final project for the Spring 2024 CITY B328: Applied GIS Projects class, taught by Professor Jennifer Hurley, which was structured around the execution of a semester-long independent Geographic Information Systems (GIS) project using ArcGIS Pro.

This website is intended to act as an archive showcasing the results of my GIS analysis, which focused on locating and analyzing the distribution of potential greyfields--what I define for the purposes of this analysis as abandoned and/or underutilized retail, industrial, and other non-residential property with high impermeable surface cover[1]--located in Fairfax, Virginia (both Fairfax County and the city of Fairfax).

The content available on the website and my analysis is directed primarily toward planners and community organizers in the area interested in investing in community redevelopment as well as researchers interested in the study and/or cataloguing of greyfield properties using GIS; however, I hope it may prove useful or interesting for others!

If you would like to take a comprehensive look at the entire project by moving through the website after reading this initial "About" page, I recommend doing so in the following order.

  1. "Process", which contains a data dictionary for the project, a list of data sources, a description of my process of indentifying potential greyfield sites, and an evaluation of my method's accuracy.
  2. "Findings", a summary of the primary findings of the project.
  3. "Exploration", which contextualizes the findings of the project by providing further analysis of the distribution of identified parcels throughout the county.
  4. "Reflections", a series of reflections both the limitations and potential expansions to the analysis as well as challenges I faced during the project, things I learned, and sub-sections of the project I undertook, but ended up scrapping.

To illustrate my findings, I have included various interactive maps of them created using ArcGIS Online.
